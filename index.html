Brief History

Trivium was founded in 1999 when original lead singer and bassist Brad Lewter saw Matt Heafy play Metallica's "No Leaf Clover" at his high school talent show, then asked him to try out for his band as lead guitar. The pair went to drummer Travis Smith's house, and impressed with his performance accepted him into Trivium.

However, two weeks after Heafy joined, Brad Lewter left the band right before a performance. Heafy took over vocal duty, and with a fill-in bass player from another local band Trivium played some successful shows while searching for a permanent bassist.

In late 2002, Trivium entered the studio to record their debut album, Ember to Inferno with Heafy writing the lyrics, as well as recording the vocals and guitar all at 16 years old. After the album was recorded, lead guitarist Corey Beaulieu joined, then in 2004 Paolo Gregoletto joined as the permanent bassist. With this line-up their album Ember to Inferno managed to get the interest of Roadrunner Records who signed them to the label that they're still on.
